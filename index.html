What is Photography?

The word "photography", like almost every other word in the English dictionary, is based on the Greek φῶς (phōs), (genitive: phōtós) meaning "light", and γραφή (graphê), meaning "drawing, writing", together meaning "drawing with light".

The forerunner of the photographic camera was camera obscura (bottom left). Camera Obscura, Latin for "Dark Room", is a phonomenon that happens when a scene on one side of the room, is projected through a small hole in the wall. What this does is it creates a inverted image on the other side of the room. This will only work if the only light in the room is coming from that hole.
